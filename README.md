## Project Background

Zara operates one of the largest fast fashion catalogs in the world, and every product decision, from where an item sits on the sales floor to whether it goes on discount, has a measurable effect on performance. Understanding those effects at scale is what separates guesswork from strategy.

## Business Problem

Zara wanted to understand the key drivers of sales volume across its product categories, including material, display position, promotions, seasonal trends and gender preference, in order to optimize product placement, marketing strategy and inventory management to drive profitability and customer satisfaction.

## Project Objectives

- Determine which product sub category generates the highest sales volume
- Evaluate the influence of product display position on sales volume
- Analyze the effect of promotions and discounts on product sales
- Identify gender based preferences across product sub categories
- Explore preferred materials by sub category and by season
- Uncover top performing products for restocking guidance

## Tools Used

`Python` · `Power BI` · `DAX`

## Analytical Process

1. **Data Cleaning** — Checked for nulls and duplicates, renamed columns for clarity, dropped non analytical fields, and added a computed revenue column
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis** — Explored sales volume by category, display position, promotion, season, material and gender in Python
3. **Power BI Visualization** — Built a single page dashboard styled in Zara's black and white brand identity, with interactive slicers for gender and promotion

Total revenue sits at **$884.56M** across 22.22M units sold and 20,250 products, with an average price of $41.95.

Jackets generate the highest revenue of any sub category at $487.02M, more than double the next closest category. Products displayed in the aisle outperform end cap and front of store placement, and sales peak sharply in autumn before falling to their lowest point in summer. A small group of hero products, led by the Plaid Overshirt, sell far above the company average and account for a disproportionate share of revenue.

## Key Insights & Recommendations

### Product Performance

**Insight:** Jackets dominate every other sub category in both sales volume and revenue, while a small set of hero products consistently outsell the rest of the catalog.

**Recommendations:** Restock top performing products ahead of others, replicate their styling and materials in future collections, and use them as a benchmark for what a strong performing product looks like.

### Display Strategy

**Insight:** Aisle placement consistently outperforms end cap and front of store, suggesting visibility and foot traffic in that zone convert more reliably into sales.

**Recommendations:** Prioritize aisle space for best selling products, and rotate underperforming items into that zone periodically to test whether placement alone can lift their numbers.

### Promotions

**Insight:** Discounted products did not consistently outsell full price products, which suggests promotions are not the blanket sales driver they are often assumed to be.

**Recommendations:** Reserve discounts for slow moving or seasonal stock instead of applying them broadly, and protect the pricing of top performing products to preserve margin.

### Seasonal & Material Trends

**Insight:** Autumn drives the highest sales volume of any season, led by wool and wool blend materials, while spring and summer lean toward cotton and linen.

**Recommendations:** Align sourcing and production schedules with seasonal material demand, and avoid overstocking materials that historically underperform in a given season.

## Conclusion

Product visibility, promotional strategy, seasonal timing and material choice all measurably shape how well a product sells. Jackets carry the brand, aisle placement outperforms every other display position, and demand shifts predictably with the seasons. Applying these insights gives Zara a clear path to sharper merchandising decisions, better inventory planning, and stronger margins without needing to guess.
